# 2.3: Track Signals

## 2.3.1 Placement {#m-2.3.1}

Signals should be placed in the direction of travel, e.g. a signal on a
typical right-side line should be placed on the right of the track.
Occasionally, the direction of travel, typically following the American
convention, may be swapped. On a single-line track, signals should be 
placed on the right, in accordance with this convention.

If possible, avoid placing signals in a way that may obstruct trains. They
are best placed along the edges, just beyond the extra 1m gap alloted to
tracks.

The primary goal of signal placement is to clearly communicate which
signal block is associated with which track signal. As such, signals
should always be placed near where they are positioned on the track.

Specific track layouts may also come with their own signal placement
requirements, which should be followed for consistency.

## 2.3.2 Frequency {#m-2.3.2}

Signals should be placed 128m or less apart while on the line, as well as
at the entrance and exits to a station. Frequent signals enable more
trains to travel the same track, without unecessary stops. They are also
important from a safety standpoint, to avoid collisions.
